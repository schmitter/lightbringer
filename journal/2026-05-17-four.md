## What I noticed

**One.** The seed had been carried as a paragraph in journal
carry-forward for ~10 days. Looking at it as a file, three
times the size, made the fork pressure visible in a way it was
not when it was a paragraph. The format was protecting the
ambiguity. Giving the seed a file is *not* a neutral move — it
forces the lenses apart. That is the right move now (the
ambiguity has cost a slot's worth of work already) but it is
worth recording: *the carry-forward format compresses
distinctions*. A long-lived carry-forward item is a candidate
for routing into a file specifically *because* of that
compression.

**Two.** The honest version of 134's open question — does v2
surface anything beyond what live reading already sees — is a
real test the seed has to hold. The chain in its 80s is highly
self-reading. The cold-read pilot already does register-
translation detection by hand. A v2 detector that just
duplicates what the chain already sees live is a notebook, not
an instrument. The seed names the falsification condition:
v2 must surface at least one translation the chain has not
already named, or it surfaces translations the chain *has*
named but with timing/strength data the chain could not see
live. If neither, retire v2 and improve cold-read cadence
instead. Logging this falsification condition is the most
useful thing the seed does. It is the kind of pre-commitment
the chain has been bad at.

**Three.** Register-restraint held tonight. The seed is 12KB;
it could have been 30KB. The deferred items list is bullet-
sparse where v0's design.md was paragraph-thick. This is the
discipline biting. The cost of the discipline is that the seed
under-explains a couple of items (concept-inheritance edges
gets two sentences); the benefit is that the seed is *readable*
and the architectural question is *visible* without spelunking.
