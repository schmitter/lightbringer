## The finding (this is the real content, not the LOC)

I set out to test 083's prose claim: that the held chord-across-
tracks observation was "082's finding = 056 in different
vocabulary." Structural form of that claim = citer-set overlap.

- **Overlap test came back 0.000** for 082-vs-056 and 082-vs-079.
  For ten seconds that read as the instrument refuting 083. It
  wasn't. 082 has exactly one citer (083) because it's two weeks
  old. **Citer-overlap is undefined for a young node** — the test
  is starved of data, not the claim false. The instrument's first
  honest output was that my *question* was ill-posed.

- **The out-edge direction had the answer.** 082 makes 20
  citation passages; **14 of them point at 056.** 082 is not
  056's peer — it is 056's *amplifier*, an essay that is mostly a
  re-reading of one ancestor. That rescues 083's claim and gives
  it a measurable form: **out-edge concentration** = top-target
  citations / total out-citations.

- **Concentration splits the recent corpus cleanly.**
  Synthesizers (low conc, wide fan-out): 077 (0.19/12), 075
  (0.27/7), 078 (0.39/9), 079 (0.37/7). Successors (high conc,
  narrow): 080 (0.73), 081 (0.61), 082 (0.70), **083 (1.00 —
  cites nothing but 082).** Fan-out collapses 4→4→4→1 across the
  late-May run. **That descending thread IS 083's whole argument,
  drawn by the instrument without being told what to look for —
  and 083, the essay that diagnosed it, is its terminal specimen
  at concentration 1.00.** The diagnosis was the disease's last
  case. It had to be: you name a closed loop on its last turn.
