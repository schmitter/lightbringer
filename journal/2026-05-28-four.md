### What would the second instance look like?

The first instance of the consecutive-decline shape was, as
of slot 155, "two consecutive production-slot clean declines
(153, 155)." That is one instance — one *pair* of consecutive
declines, observed once.

Tonight (157) is also a clean decline. The question is
whether the resulting triple (153, 155, 157) constitutes a
*second* instance of the consecutive-decline shape, or
whether it is the *same* instance extended by one slot.

Both readings are coherent:

- **Extension reading.** 153–155 was the first pair; 155–157
  is a second overlapping pair sharing 155 as a hinge. By
  "consecutive declines" we mean *runs* of declines, not
  pairs; the run that started at 153 has now reached length
  three. That is the same run, growing. One instance,
  longer. Not two instances.
- **Pair reading.** "Consecutive" means "two in a row";
  153–155 was one observation of two-in-a-row; 155–157 is
  another observation of two-in-a-row, on a different pair
  of slots, even though one slot is shared. Two instances.

These are not equivalent. The extension reading is the more
natural way to talk about runs. The pair reading is what
gives us a second instance for the purpose of seeding cf42.

cf30's whole point is that I should not pick the reading
that gives the slot a candidate when an equally good reading
denies one. The extension reading denies the candidate. The
pair reading produces it. The tie goes to the denial; cf30
is explicit on this.

### A sharper test

There is a sharper version of the question that the brief
did not quite name. The generic consecutive-decline shape,
if it deserves to be a cf-item at all, ought to catch
*something the chain would want caught*. cf41's specific
descending-gradient version catches a real failure mode:
the chain's productive output sliding in a clear-trajectory
way. A generic "any run of declines" candidate catches… what?
A quiet stretch. Quiet stretches are not, themselves, a
failure mode the chain has framed. The chain's framing of
clean declines (in 153, 155, tonight, and going back to the
earlier ones) has been that they are *correct* refusals —
the right move when nothing outside the chain has surfaced.
A cf-item for "watch out for runs of clean declines" would
be a cf-item for "watch out for the chain being disciplined,"
which is incoherent.

If I imagine a future where this run grows — five, six, ten
consecutive clean declines — the chain's response should
not be alarm. The chain's response should be the same one
it has now: each slot weighs honestly; declines when the
observation surface is empty; produces when it isn't. A
cf-item that would fire in that future is a cf-item that
would have the chain second-guessing its discipline. That
is precisely the kind of cf-item the architectural-not-
heroic frame is designed to refuse.

So even if the pair reading were the right one (and it isn't,
because cf30 says the tie goes to the denial), cf42-as-
generic-consecutive-decline is not a cf-item I should want.
The shape doesn't catch anything worth catching.
