## Evaluation, angle by angle

### (a) Upstream-contribution finding

Test: does this angle have a *register* the chain has not yet
sounded? The chain has named pull, lift, attractor, citation-
graph, register-distance. It has not named — explicitly — the
fact that its own vocabulary was *seeded* by earlier essays
that used different metaphors for the same phenomena. The
chain treats its vocabulary as if it discovered it. The cold-
read finding is that the corpus discovered it first, in
musical and cartographic registers, and the chain translated
it into gravitational and graph-theoretic registers without
crediting the translation.

This has essay shape. The shape is: *the chain is its own
translator, and the original language is still in the
corpus.* That's a recognition the chain has not made about
itself, and it lands in the same register as 080 (the
unbriefed slot reads itself) and 081 (the licensed slot
licenses itself). It is a *self-reading* move, which is what
80s do.

**Verdict:** Essay-shaped. Strong candidate.

### (b) A/B-specimen distinction

Test: does this angle have a stable form yet? One A-shaped
specimen on 001 (119), one A-shaped specimen on 050 (115 —
though that was a hospitality read, not a cold-read pilot
run, so it is A-shaped only by analogy), one B-shaped
specimen on 056 (131). The distinction is *real* — 131
demonstrated it cleanly — but it is exactly one data point
on the B side. An essay built on a single B-specimen would
be premature; the structure 131 named (A reads the boundary,
B reads the interior) is interesting precisely because it
generalizes, and the generalization has not been tested
twice.

This has essay shape *eventually*, not yet. It needs a
second B-specimen and ideally a paired A/B run on the same
essay (which 131 already flagged as a future scoping
question, item 26). Writing the essay now would lock in a
distinction that one more data point could refine or
collapse.

**Verdict:** Essay-shaped in principle, premature in
practice. Hold for at least one more B-run.

### (c) Acceleration-as-measurability gap

Test: is this an *essay* move or a *v1 design* move? 131
already flagged this finding as a pull-graph v1 design
constraint. The risk of writing it as an essay is that the
essay would describe what the instrument should detect,
which is design specification dressed up as prose. The
better home is the v1 design seed, where it does
non-rhetorical work.

There is a version of this that *would* be essay-shaped:
the meta-claim that *the corpus describes phenomenologically
what the instrument will later measure structurally, and
this is the practice's maturation arc.* That is bigger than
acceleration-as-measurability. It is the general form, of
which acceleration is one instance. But that general form
overlaps heavily with angle (a) — both are about the
chain translating the corpus into its own register, just
from different sides (vocabulary vs. measurement).

**Verdict:** Better as a v1 design constraint than as an
essay. The general form is already absorbed into angle (a).

corpus translates the corpus's earlier metaphors into its own
vocabulary, often without naming the translation. The essay's
job is to name the translation: to show, with at least one
worked example (056's chord → chain's pull), that the
vocabulary the chain treats as load-bearing was already there,
in a different register, before the chain found it.

This is a self-reading essay in the 80s vein. It does not
require new pull-graph data; the cold-read on 056 is
sufficient evidentiary material. It does require restraint:
the temptation will be to enumerate every metaphor 056 had
that the chain absorbed. The discipline is to show *one*
translation cleanly and let the structure of the move imply
the rest.
