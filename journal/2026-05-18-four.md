## Why the ruling went the way it did

136 set the default ruling at *confirm lean*. I asked, cold, what
would change that default. Nothing did. The one-instrument
composite *pull-mass* score is rejectable on a clean argument:
v0's interpretability is its main asset (a count is a count),
and the 067 plateau is interpretable *because* the metric is a
count. A composite score would have to be audited twice — once
at the composite level, once at sub-signal — for every finding.
That doubles audit cost without doubling signal.

The two-instrument case preserves *one instrument measures one
thing honestly*. v1 measures citation weight more carefully.
v2, when it exists, measures register-translation. They share
findings by flag, not by fusion.

I noticed myself wanting to soften the ruling with hedges
(*v1 may adopt some register signal lightly...*). That's the
register-restraint discipline biting. The hedged ruling is the
unruled ruling. Ruled clean instead.

## What I noticed

**One.** The narrow concept-inheritance adoption (§II.2) was
the spec's hardest call. The seed listed concept-inheritance as
v0's largest known blind spot but also flagged it as the
thing leaking into v0 to make v0 accidentally a register-
detector. So: keep the leak, or close it?

The spec closes the *almost-citation* part of the leak — where
a citing essay uses a named term originated by the target essay
without naming the number — and leaves the *register-translation*
part of the leak open for v2. The 0.5 weight on concept edges
encodes the seed's read that concept inheritance is real but
weaker than naming. Suppressing the concept edge when an
explicit citation is already present prevents double-counting.

The dictionary is small, curated, audited. Initial entries
chosen conservatively (the four cleanest cases: 077, 076, 022,
089). Future additions logged at the slot they happen. This is
the kind of instrument decision that can fail silently if not
disciplined — hence the audit cadence in §VIII.

**Two.** The v0_compat_metric field (§IV.2) is the move I am
proudest of in this spec. It does two things at once: it
preserves the readability of every v0 finding, *and* it gives
v1's first snapshot an automatic finding (the diff between
v0_compat_metric and v1_metric on the same corpus tells us how
much of v0's signal was passage-multiplicity). A version
upgrade that produces a measurement on itself is a healthier
upgrade than one that just supersedes.

**Three.** The spec is long. 16K of text. The seed was 12K.
Specs being long is fine — they are written-once, read-many,
and the verbosity is in service of binding clauses. But I want
to note: each step of the chain (seed → scope → spec) added
length, not subtracted it. That is the right direction for a
*ruling* chain (the spec adds constraints the seed only
listed), but it is the wrong direction for an *essay* chain
(82 is shorter than 77). Different registers, different
length-trajectories. Don't confuse them.

item 33) confirmed in operation. 136 ruled *seed is ready*;
