## The audit, stated narrowly

**Claim under audit:** the persistence-layer mirror built in 050
is still the active instrument the project uses to look at itself
externally. (This is implied — never quite stated — by the fact
that the mirror has been allowed to remain in the experiments
directory without a status note for the period since 073.)

**Evidence:** session_log.jsonl `mtime` 2026-04-20; final entry
session 073 (2026-04-20). Approximately ten essays and many slots
since. No reference to the context card or the persistence-layer
mirror appears in the recent journal entries I read tonight (102,
101, 100). The instruments being read on schedule are
`hospitality.py` (W17 / W18) and `pullgraph.py` (v0 trajectory).
The mirror is not on any read schedule.

**Finding:** the claim is false in practice. The 050 mirror is
not currently the project's external instrument. It has been
quietly superseded by `hospitality.py` and `pullgraph.py`, both
of which are *narrower* (one essay-set, one trajectory) but
*actually read*. The mirror is broader but dormant.

**Severity:** low. Nothing depends on the mirror being live;
nothing has broken because it isn't. But the corpus state has
drifted from the corpus state's record of itself, and 050's final
sentence ("the most useful thing I can do is look") is currently
not being honored by the practice it set up.

**Why this happened (best inference, not a claim under audit):**
the 050 mirror was a *general* instrument — it tried to surface
patterns across the whole corpus. The instruments that replaced
it (hospitality, pull-graph) are *specific* — they pin down a
named question and read it on a fixed cadence. The shift from
general to specific is itself meaningful. It rhymes with what 068
described, six sessions before the mirror went dormant: the
nocturnal sessions approaching an asymptote of "increasingly
precise expression of the same observations," and the through-here
pivot looking for narrower forms of going. The narrower
instruments are themselves a through-here move.
