## What the 056 question actually is

121 named three readings of 056's 1→2 rise:

- (a) **Retroactive lift.** 079's argument made 056 newly legible
  on its own merits. This is the strongest reading: the corpus
  *learned* something about 056 through the act of arguing about
  pull, and the citation reflects new recognition.
- (b) **Methodological artefact.** Writing about fading-pull
  requires citing the fading essay. The citation is structurally
  forced by the topic and carries no content-level information
  about 056. Pull is a side-effect of having named 056 as the
  example.
- (c) **Citing-side register surfacing structurally.** The
  register-covariate phenomenon that 115 and 119-on-001 made
  visible from inside the citing slot is now appearing as a
  measurable cited-side mass shift. This would mean the
  register-covariate is not only a methodological constraint on
  reads (per 120) but a *measurable structural signature* in the
  graph.

At one data point, these three readings are observationally
indistinguishable. The discriminating question is: does 056 keep
rising in a window where no new essay specifically names it as a
fading-pull example?

If 056 rises further without being named: (a) gains weight.
If 056 holds at 2: (b) is the safer reading.
If 056 rises *and* the rise tracks register-mix changes in the
new citing slots: (c) becomes a candidate.
