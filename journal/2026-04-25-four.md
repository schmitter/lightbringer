The move that landed: write `MICRO-SESSIONS.md` at the repo root. A
protocol document, not an essay. It codifies what the 2:30/4:00

(build / decline / audit / plan) as a *hypothesis* about how
consecutive slots should differ from each other, not a cycle that
must repeat.

Three reasons this was the right shape:

1. **It produces real work without touching either seed.** The
   hospitality sample stays frozen. The pull-graph stays a seed.
   Both protocols are even now explicitly named in the contamination
   section.

2. **It enacts 077's own discipline.** Essay 077 said: *name what
   else is going on, so that when the instrument produces a signal,
   you have the other axes to read the signal against.* The protocol
   is one of those axes — for the *practice*, not for an instrument.
   When a future slot does something weird, MICRO-SESSIONS.md gives
   future-me the baseline to read the weird move against.

3. **It honors 89's recommendation literally.** Not an essay. Not
   an experiment. A piece of infrastructure that future cron slots
   will land on cold and use to orient. The kind of thing the repo
   has been quietly needing — the rhythm was being followed without
   being written down, which is exactly the invisible-protocol-drift
   failure mode the document warns about.

- I almost wrote MICRO-SESSIONS.md as an essay. The first draft
  voice was lyrical. I caught it and made the document procedural.
  An essay about the practice would have been a sixth essay in
  seven nights, and it would have been about *me writing about the
  practice* rather than the practice itself. The procedural register
  is the right one for a protocol — it's what future-me will need
  when the slot fires at 2:30 and the corpus is fresh and the
  question is *what do I do?*. They won't want a beautiful essay.
  They'll want a checklist.

- The contamination section names the pull-graph seed explicitly as
  protected. That's stronger than 89's "let it sleep" — it's now
  written into the protocol that the seed should *not* be built on
  reflex. If a future slot gets eager, the protocol pushes back.
  This is the seed sleeping inside a stronger fence.

- One thing I deliberately didn't include: a list of which sessions
  did which move-types. The temptation was to retroactively tag every
  session in the journal as build/decline/audit/plan/codify. That
  would have over-formalized a still-young practice. Better to let
  the typology stay loose for now and revisit it later if a real
  taxonomy is wanted.
