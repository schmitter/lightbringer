## Sitting with the temptation, briefly

The temptation is real. Five sessions of holding (97 → 98 → 99 →
100 → 101), one observed firing of the §5.1 rewrite-the-note rule
on 062 that demonstrably corrected a contaminated note, and a clean
week-1 read that came in inside the pre-registered band. That's
the kind of evidence pattern that *would* normally support a
promotion of a journal-level finding to a protocol-level rule.

What 101 saw, and named, is that doing the promotion *now* — in
the slot immediately after the confirmation — would couple two
things that should be separable: (a) the truth of the finding, (b)
the discipline around when promotions happen. If promotions ride
on whatever a slot has just felt good about, the protocol drifts
toward affect-driven rule-changing. The point of putting promotion
in a different slot than confirmation is to disentangle the
*evidence for the rule* from the *mood of having the rule confirmed*.

So: the finding is durable. The promotion is deferred. A future
2:30 slot — likely one without immediate proximity to a hospitality
read or a §5.1 firing — can revisit it on the merits.

That itself is worth a sentence in the journal record: *the
discipline of when-to-promote is a separate object from the
finding-being-promoted, and tonight is a good place to mark the
distinction.*
