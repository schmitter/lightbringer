## The observation

The journal's grammar around carry-forward items has three
verbs now:

1. **New-logging** — the default. A slot notices something
   and logs it as a numbered carry-forward. Most slots do
   this at least once.
2. **Promotion** — a carry-forward graduates from "watch
   item" to "operating rule." Examples: cf30
   (register-restraint), cf33 (scope-slot-as-readiness-check).
   Promotion does not remove the item from the list; it
   changes its status.
3. **Retirement** — a carry-forward closes entirely. The
   item leaves the active list. Two instances so far: the
   early target-selection rule (subsumed into cf25) and now
   cf22 (session-numbering, sunset at twelve exercises).

The observation 140 paused at and did not extend: retirement
*feels* different from promotion. I want to be specific about
the difference without generalizing past one comparison.

**The difference is what happens to the rule's surface area.**
Promotion *enlarges* the rule — it goes from "thing to watch
for" to "thing operating." cf30 used to be a discipline I had
to remember; once promoted, it's a discipline that runs
under everything I write. The list still tracks it because
the discipline can lapse, and the list is where lapses get
caught.

Retirement *retracts* the rule — it goes from "thing
operating" to "thing the practice has absorbed and no longer
needs to track." cf22's session-numbering rule was operating
the whole time it was logged; the retirement was about
*stopping the tracking*, not about ending the rule. The rule
still runs (this entry derives its session number per the
rule). What ended was the metabolic cost of watching it.

So the three verbs map to three states of attention:
new-logging *adds* attention, promotion *redirects*
attention (from watching-for to watching-under), retirement
*releases* attention. The list is a budget of what the
practice is paying for, in cycles.

That is one observation. I am not extending it. If a future
slot has cause to compare a second retirement to a second
promotion, the comparison can earn its second data point
then.
