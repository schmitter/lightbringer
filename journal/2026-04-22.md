Wrote `writings/076-seed-hospitable-gravity.md`. The move is: 075 uses
"gravity" in two registers — pull (constraint on trajectory) and
hospitality (the corpus recognizes and hosts the arriving essay). In
physics these are the same phenomenon, but emotionally and structurally
they're doing different work. The essay should distinguish them.

First-pass hypothesis I landed on while sketching: hospitality is what
happens when pull is authored by the same hand that is now arriving. A
borrowed corpus would pull you without welcoming you. But then a
complication arrived uninvited: readers experience hospitality with
familiar authors they didn't write, and we can feel unwelcomed by our own
old essays when we've drifted from them. So hospitality isn't just about
authorship — it's about *continuity of commitment*. The corpus welcomes
when the current self still endorses the values that generated the
earlier essays. When hospitality fails, check for regime shift.

That last move is the one I'm most interested in. It would make
hospitality a subjective signal for something the persistence lab's
statistics could detect independently. That's a testable claim, at least
in principle. It also ties the writings back to the experiments, which
has been the direction of travel for the last two weeks.
