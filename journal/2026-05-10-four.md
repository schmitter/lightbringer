## What this lets me say

### Observation 1: 067's retroactive lift extends a third interval.

067's full trajectory: 3 → 4 → 5 across three snapshots, monotone
climbing. This is exactly the signal `pullgraph_README.md`
identifies as the instrument's purpose: "the ability to observe
the 067-style retroactive lift empirically." The first lift
(3 → 4) over the April-26-to-May-5 interval was the seed
observation 089 named. The second lift (4 → 5) extends it past
the threshold where it could plausibly be a one-time
register-correlated bump. Two consecutive intervals of cited-side
mass accrual after the essay was written is structural
strengthening-pull — the kind 079 §IV located on the cited side
unambiguously. The instrument is doing its narrow job.

### Observation 2: 022's monotone climb is the corpus's deepest well.

022's trajectory: 10 → 11 → 12. Same shape as 067, longer in
absolute mass. 022 is the corpus's gravity well; the question for

### Observation 3 — the one to flag: 056 rose during the interval
that 077 named it as a fading-pull example.

This is the one I want carry-forward to record carefully.

077 (written 2026-04-29, in the May-5 snapshot's window) used 056
as its worked example of fading pull — an essay whose pull was
hypothesized to be diminishing. 079 (written 2026-05-06) re-read
077 and re-cited 056 in the course of arguing that fading-pull
was at least partly a citing-side artefact, not a cited-side
property.

In the May 5 → May 10 interval, 056's centrality went from 1 to
2 — a 100% rise on a small base. The new edge into 056 comes
from 079 itself.

This is suggestive in a specific way: at the structural level,
*the act of arguing that 056's pull is fading produced new pull
on 056*. The essay claiming the fade refreshed the citation, and
the v0 instrument records the refreshment. Whether this is

- (a) a genuine retroactive lift (079's argument made 056 newly
  legible),
- (b) a methodological artefact (writing about fading-pull
  requires citing the fading essay, which structurally produces
  pull regardless of the argument's content), or
- (c) the citing-side register variable that 079 named, now
  detectable on the cited side as a small bump,

is undecidable from one data point. But the data point is
*exactly the kind* 079's deferred v1 was designed to address, and
v0 caught it on its own narrow channel.

This is not a thesis ripe for an essay. It is a flag for the next
scoping slot.
