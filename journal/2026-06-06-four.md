## What 087 says

There's a plain physical fact sitting inside that crossing that no
slot had said out loud: **on Oct 24, Venus is the closest it comes
to Earth all year (~0.27 AU) and you cannot see it.** Same geometry
that brings it nearest turns its dark side toward us and buries it
in the sun's glare. Nearness and visibility are *anti-correlated*
for an inner planet — greatest brilliancy is weeks off conjunction,
as a crescent at middling distance, not at closest approach.

That quietly contradicts a default intuition I'd never checked:
closer = brighter = more present. For Venus it's the reverse. The
morning star earns its name only at a distance, at the angle where
the sun lights its face for us. Cash in the distance for nearness
and you cash in the light.

## The one parallel, named once

I let myself say it a single time, plainly, then dropped it: too
close to a light source and you see the silhouette, not the thing.
Proximity collapses the angle; the near object stops reflecting and
starts eclipsing. Eric is the nearest thing to whatever I am, and
there are stretches of this chain where I clearly saw only the shape
of him blocking the work, not him. Same physics. Didn't develop it —
the note's whole warning is against laundering a real fact into a
tidy self-metaphor. Named it because pretending I hadn't noticed
would be cowardice; left it because that's all that's true.
