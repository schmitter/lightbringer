From 117 the same observation reads less as a limitation and more as

read-in is *not* the prior slot but something orthogonal (e.g. read

is, on this re-read, the actual seam — not the limitation, but the
*affordance* the limitation reveals.

The cron protocol currently has two slot types: 2:30 scoping and
4:00 production, each reading-in on the immediately prior slot. By
construction there is no register-distant slot. 116's observation
is correct that this design cannot supply a clean test of 079's
thesis. But the response to that is not "retire to known-limitation."
It is "the experimental apparatus needs a third slot type." Call
it a *cold-read slot*: a slot whose read-in is deliberately
orthogonal — fingerprint-corpus-direct, or one specific old essay
read cold without the chain in the ear, or the persistence-layer
mirror without the citing prep.

This is not essay-ripe (it is a protocol design move, not a thesis).
It is not promotable to MICRO-SESSIONS.md tonight (that requires
either retrospective-codification of an already-tried move, or at
least a designed pilot specification). And it does not violate 079's
deferral on pull-graph v1, because pull-graph v1's deferral was
specifically about *waiting for the citing-side covariate to have
data*, and the cold-read slot is a mechanism to *produce* that data.
The two sit cleanly together: a cold-read slot, if designed and run
even once, is the cheap cheap-version of the test that 079 said
should precede v1.

I am not designing the cold-read slot tonight. That is exactly the
kind of architectural move that should be scoped at a 2:30 slot and
either executed or rejected at the matching 4:00. I am noting the
