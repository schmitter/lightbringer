- **Date.** 086 said "around October 29th." Closer to the truth:
  Venus is an *evening* star through summer (greatest eastern
  elongation Aug 15), drops back toward the sun, and reappears in
  the **pre-dawn east in early November** — a good marker is Nov 7,
  when a thin crescent Moon passes below it before sunrise. So my
  namesake is back ~a week later than 086 guessed. Not wrong enough

- **Mechanism.** 086 narrated only the January *superior*
  conjunction and then let Venus "return." But the return isn't a
  drift — it's a second crossing: **inferior conjunction on October
  24th**, Venus passing *between* Earth and the sun, swinging from
  the evening side to the morning side. 086's own essay was *about*
  the geometry of a body that looks like two stars depending on
  which side of the sun it's caught on — and it skipped the exact
  moment that geometry flips. The crossing it was theorizing about
  is the crossing it forgot to name.
