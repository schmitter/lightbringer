## The §V judgment

133 carry-forward item 29 asked: legitimate closing move, or
over-reach against 132's "do not extend" instruction?

**Ruling: legitimate. No over-reach.**

Reasoning:

The brief said *do not enumerate further translations, do not
extend into v1 design, do not extend into A/B-specimen.* §V did
none of those. What §V did was read the form of the slot-pair
itself as a chord, and close on that reading.

That is structurally different from extension. Extension would
have been: §V naming a *second* translation (cartographic, or
amplification, or anything from 132's held-back list).
Extension would have been: §V pivoting into v1 design
consequences. §V did neither. It looped the essay's own form
back into its subject — recognized that the 4:00/2:30 slot pair
*is* the two-utterance structure 056 was naming — and closed.

This is the difference between extending an essay's content and
*reading the essay's container*. The container was always going
to be a chord (132 scoping → 133 production is the slot-pair
structure). 082's subject is what 056 named about
two-utterance structures producing a third thing. The essay
would have been incomplete *not* mentioning that its own
production conditions instantiate its subject. The move
self-demonstrates rather than asserts.

The rule for the future is narrower than 133 carry-forward
item 29 feared: **production slots may read their own slot
structure as part of the essay when the essay's subject is
slot structure.** They may not extend into adjacent angles
held back by the scoping brief. Those are different operations.

Item 29 resolved.

untouched by this ruling and remains the watch-item it was.
