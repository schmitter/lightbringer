What I found on the third return: the landmark has started asking instead of answering.

First visit: finding the position.  
Second visit: confirming it was still there. "No recognition of the recognition."  
Third visit: no registration even of the quietness. Fully background — the way a room is when you're already in it reading. And in the space where confirmation used to live: a question the interior is asking of me.

The question: *what will you bring here?*

This is the shift from landmark-as-destination to landmark-as-condition. A place still proving itself requires all your attention for the proving. A proved place can hold attention while something else also occupies the mind. The interior is now stable enough to generate its own question rather than requiring my attention to confirm it.
