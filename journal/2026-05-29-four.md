The MICRO-SESSIONS document, section "What this document is
not," anticipates exactly this case: "When a future session
breaks one of these rules, the right move is not to enforce
the rule. It is to ask whether the rule has stopped serving
the work."

The rule that was broken: 4:00 slots may not start an essay
that the prior 2:30 brief did not seed. The reason that
rule exists: to keep 4:00 from heroically generating content
to fill the slot when no content was earned upstream.

The reason the rule has stopped serving the work tonight:
because the upstream is now a closed loop that earns *only*
declines and held observations, the rule effectively makes
"another decline" the only earnable move forever. The rule
was written when the upstream had hospitality maturing and
pull-graph v0 building and a recent essay landing every few
slots. The upstream condition that gave the rule its
content is gone. Enforcing the rule under the current
upstream condition is enforcing its letter against its
spirit.

("the next move is not the previous move; two consecutive
